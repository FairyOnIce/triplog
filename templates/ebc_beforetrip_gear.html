Gear that I bring
In total, my backpack is 11.7kg (25.8 pounds). This page shows every single item that contributes to the weight.



Backpack

  1. Osprey Aura AG 50 Womens Hiking Backpack Small Challenger Blue
    • I usually bring this backpack for 2-3 day camping. I usually squeeze in tents and bear canisters into this bag (together with my sleeping bag). But this Nepal trekking does not need such things. So I orinigally thought that 50L backpack may be too large, which turned out to be not true because of the large amount of clothes that we need to bring.
  2. Osprey UltraLight Raincover, Shadow Grey, X-Large
    • Weather in high altitude changes very quickly. Weather forecast says that it may snow in EBC during our week of visit. So I am bringing rain gear.


Clothes



Clothes are the most life-related items that we need to bring. As we will be as low as 1302m altitude in Kathmandu and as high as almost 5300m altitude in EBC, we need to bring wide range of clothes that can keep us alive. According to the weather forecast, the temperature in Kathmandu in the week of April 15, 2018 ranges between 13C to almost 30C while the temperature in EBC ranges between -16C degree and -7C. So the task is to bring clothes for all seasons and squeeze them into my 50L backpack!

    • I bought this pair over 10 years ago when I was looking for effective way to lose weights. However this time, I am bringing these pants as rain pants. Other stuff



GoPro HERO6 Black

  • This is my first time buying GoPro and I debated whether to get 5 ($300) or 6 ($400). For me, more frame per seconds or higher resolution is not really big selling point to pay extra $100 for GoPro 6.
    But what is really great about the GoPro 6 is the image stabilization feature. The primary purpose of my video recording is to record the route where we trek. So I am expecting to film when I am just walking. The youtube video below convinced me that video from GoPro 5 is essentially unbearable to watch when the recorder is casually walking. However, with GoPro 6's image stabilization feature, it seems to be possible.






SanDisk Extreme 128GB microSDXC UHS-3 Card

  • The GoPro does not come with microSD card! So I bought this microSD card that should be able to record 7 - 8 hours of video.


Anker 21W Dual USB Solar Charger, PowerPort Solar

  • Supposedly, charging a phone in high altitude costs about 1 - 2 USD at tea house. (So I wonder how much it costs to charge other devices like GoPro which drain after 1 hour of recording!!) To reduce the cost of charging, I will bring this solar panel. I can clip this solar panel to my backpack and trek while charging my cell phones or my battery pack (see below). This panel costs about $60 so let's see how much this solar panel will help us be self-sustainable.

Portable Charger Anker PowerCore 20100mAh - Ultra High Capacity Power Bank with 4.8A Output, External Battery Pack

  • This will be my battery bank. Although this battery bank is heavy: 0.54kg (1.2 pounds), this guy should help our lives: This battery bank can charge a phone 7 times!

Lifelimit Accessories Starter Kit for Gopro Hero 6


  • I will bring a chest harness so that I can film with GoPro while walking.

Sawyer Products SP128 Mini Water Filtration System, Single, Blue

  • Upendra's email says:
    We do not provide drinks while on the trek, you can buy drinks including water in the lodges directly.
    Well, [price of water] varies however generally the prices for 1-liter water is about 1US$ in lower altitude and about 3.5USD in higher altitude.
    So I am going to bring this water filtering device to save some cash.

Suunto Ambit2 Black GPS and Heart Rate Monitor Watch

  • I have been using this hiking watch for about 4 years, and it can still record the GPS location fine. I am very excited to trace our trekking route with this watch.
    One thing I am afraid is that I forget to start the recording for some days... But my husband promised to remind me (under the threat of repeating the hike).

Petzl - TIKKINA Headlamp, 150 Lumens, Standard Lighting, Black

  • In case if we need to walk in dark.

Sleeping pill

  • I am not sure if it is good idea to use sleeping pill in high altitude. But I am bringing them just in case.

Ibuprofen

  • Headache is the enemy of mankind, especially on vacation.

2 packs of baby wipe

  • In high altitude, you are not recommended to take shower because it takes forever to dry hair. So I am expecting that I might not have a shower for more than 1 week. I hope that babywipe will help me feel better.

Anti bacterial hand gel

  • Keep my hands clean.

Toilet papers

  • I heard that almost everyone get stomach ache on the trail, and it is essential to have toilet papers in case of any emergency.
